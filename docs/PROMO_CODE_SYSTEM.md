### Compliance

In case an influencer isn't being compliant and mentioning that a post is an ad or they receive commissions, can handle in phases:

To be FTC compliant, make an effort to reach out to them and mention you notice they didn't use proper labeling for their video and that we would need to pause their promo code soon if we don't address the issue

- after giving clear guidance / warning and good faith time to correct the issue, then can go to the contract terms for releasing an affiliate

Affiliate disclosure with links, need to mention relationship near link or in the url itself:  "Affiliate links are a per se material connection. Receiving a commission on a sale — regardless of the commission rate or dollar amount — materially affects reviewer credibility by definition. § 255.5 Example 11 makes this explicit: any content containing an affiliate link requires a disclosure in or before that content, every time. The size of the payout does not create an exception, and neither does the creator's follower count. FTC requirements apply to nano-influencers and micro-creators on the same terms as accounts with millions of followers." https://blog.promise.legal/ftc-endorsement-rules-product-sellers/

Key features: not burying 'sponsored' in the caption and also verbalizing it if given a verbal endorsement at the beginning, not the end
- can super-impose the relationship on the video itself, like "Ali Partner"
- can't just use the platform toggle, needs to be stated in the captions and seen or heard in the video

### Contract terms

"Agreements: Include FTC disclosure requirements in every affiliate and influencer contract, specifying approved disclosure language and platform-by-platform implementation.
Affiliate links specifically: Monitor affiliate content to confirm that disclosure language is placed near or before each affiliate link, using consumer-legible phrasing — "paid link" or "I earn a commission" qualifies; "affiliate link" alone may not, because consumers often do not know what it means.
Briefing and direction: Do not brief influencers in a way that discourages or omits disclosure. Sending free product with instructions to post, without disclosure guidance, is a compliance gap the brand owns.
Monitoring and documentation: Audit affiliate and influencer content regularly. Pre-approval workflows and documented spot-checks are baseline controls.
Insider posts: Officers, managers, and employees who post publicly about the business's products must clearly disclose their material relationship — this obligation applies to their personal social media accounts, not only brand channels." https://blog.promise.legal/ftc-endorsement-rules-product-sellers/

"Company reserves the right to terminate this Agreement immediately if the Affiliate fails to comply with FTC Endorsement Guidelines. Upon termination for non-compliance, the Affiliate’s promotional codes will be deactivated. Company will pay any undisputed commissions legally accrued prior to the date of termination, but no further commissions or recurring lifetime payouts will accrue post-termination."

"Failure to clearly disclose an affiliate relationship in accordance with FTC guidelines constitutes a material breach of contract. In the event of a material breach, Company reserves the right to terminate this agreement immediately and withhold any pending or unpaid commissions as liquidated damages to offset potential regulatory liability."
